• ? Prerequisites
  • Do all of the program prerequisites need to be completed prior to application?
    No. However, as described on the Prerequisite Courses page, the RMUoHP PA program allows applicants to have up to nine credits of prerequisite coursework in progress or planned at the time of application submission to CASPA and a feasible plan to complete any outstanding prerequisite coursework must be documented in the CASPA application. Furthermore, applicants offered seats contingent upon successful completion of outstanding prerequisites must complete each remaining course with a grade of “B” or better.
    I completed a one semester course for Anatomy and Physiology. Will this work?
    No. To meet the Human Anatomy and Physiology prerequisite requirement, applicants must take two courses, one for Human Anatomy and one for Human Physiology. A two-semester combined Human Anatomy and Physiology course series is also acceptable. These courses should be those required of majors in Biology or Physiology. Animal-based anatomy and physiology courses are not acceptable, even if you are majoring in Biology and your major does not require human anatomy and physiology.
    Can a single course be used to satisfy more than one prerequisite?
    No.
    Regarding the Verbal (V) and Quantitative (Q) GRE aggregate (V+Q) score requirement of 298 and Analytical Writing (A) score requirement of 3.5, if I do not meet one of these score requirements, can I still qualify if my score surpassed the requirement in the other? For example, would I qualify with a V+Q aggregate score of 291 and an A score of 4.0?
    No. Even though the Analytical Writing score would be sufficient, the minimum must be met for both scores.
  • ? Admissions Process
  • When will your inaugural cohort begin class?
    Our inaugural cohort will begin on Monday, May 4, 2015 (pending successfully being awarded 'Accreditation - Provisional' in March 2015). To learn details regarding accreditation, please navigate to our Accreditation web page.
    Do you use the Central Application Service for Physician Assistants (CASPA)?
    Yes. Interested applicants must apply through CASPA. Click or tap here to navigate to CASPA's website.
    When will the next application cycle open?
    April 22nd, 2015. Applicants can select our program to receive their application via the CASPA website.
    What is the difference between application screening and scoring?
    Screening determines if an application qualifies for scoring. Scoring determines the strength of an application, thereby determining an applicant's likelihood of being invited for an interview.
    How many times is each application screened per cycle?
    Once. An individual's application is screened only once per cycle—after all of his/her application materials have been verified by CASPA (including GRE scores). No additional materials pertaining to screening will be considered after this point for the application cycle. For example, if GPA requirements are not met at the time of application screening, GPA scores will not be recalculated to determine if an applicant qualifies for scoring in the event additional coursework is completed. Therefore, make sure your application fully qualifies at the time you submit it via CASPA.
    Will there be a supplementary application?
    No.
    How many applicants do you expect will apply for the next cycle?
    This is difficult to predict. We had 615 applicants for the first cycle. Already there is great enthusiasm about our new program and it appears word is spreading quickly. It would not be surprising to see 800+ applicants for our second cycle.
  • ? Length of Program
  • What is the length of the entire program?
    Seven consecutive semesters spanning 28 months.
    How many semesters are didactic and how many are clinical?
    Four didactic semesters and three clinical semesters.
    I heard you will be offering a full-time clinical rotation to break up the didactic phase. Is this true? If so, why?
    Yes, it's true. Why? Because it just makes sense. This unique aspect of our program is exciting! The practice of medicine has evolved dramatically and continues to change at a rapid pace. Consequently, the educational challenge has become (much) less about memorizing mass amounts of information, and (much) more about clinical reasoning, problem-solving, and accessing, interpreting, and applying high quality evidence-based information at the point-of-care. The purpose of including a full-time clinical rotation in the midst of the didactic phase is to facilitate the clinical problem-solving emphasis. This will empower students with a substantially more mature clinical perspective for their remaining didactic content. Because students will be prepared to grasp concepts of greater complexity, faculty will be able to design and implement meatier learning experiences.
  • ? Academics and Credentials
  • What degree will be rewarded by the program?
    Upon successful completion of the program, Rocky Mountain University of Health Professions (RMUoHP) will award students with a Master of Physician Assistant Studies (MPAS) degree. A master's degree is considered the terminal practice degree for physician assistants.
    Does the program offer a part-time option?
    No. The program is a cohort-based, lock-step experience. Courses are designed and sequenced carefully so as to allow students to progressively build upon previously learned material.
    Will a cadaver lab be included as part of the Applied Human Anatomy course?
    Yes. RMUoHP employs a full-time PhD-trained anatomist who will serve as director over the lab. Four prosected cadavers will be available for laboratory learning experiences.

  • ? Background Check
  • Will a background check be required for incoming students? If so, why?
    Admittance to the program will be contingent upon a successful background check. A background check revealing information that would prohibit a student from participating in clinical rotations or prevent licensure will make him/her ineligible for the program.
  • ? Drug Screening
  • Will drug screening be performed on students?
    Yes. Enrolled students will be subject to random drug screenings at any point during the course of the program. Failure to perform or successfully pass a drug screen will render the student ineligible for clinical rotations and will thus result in dismissal from the program.
  • ? Financials and Living
  • How much will the program cost?
    At this time, the cost is $91,810 for tuition plus fees. Because RMUoHP is a private institution, the same cost applies to both in-state and out-of-state students. As of this writing (2015-02-18), the average total cost for programs nationwide (non-resident and private) was in the low to mid-80K range.
    In the answer above, why didn't you compare RMUoHP's cost against resident tuition rates?
    RMUoHP is a private university. As such, comparable programs to evaluate us against would be those housed in other private institutions. Only about 1/3 of PA programs nationwide are state institutions having the ability to offer discounted resident tuition rates.
    Will financial aid be available?
    RMUoHP participates in the Federal Direct Student Loan (Stafford/PLUS) program, commonly referred to as “federal student loans,” offered through the US Department of Education (ED). Eligibility for federally-funded financial aid is established by the US Department of Education regulations. RMUoHP has received formal approval for Title IV funding for its PA program. This means that our PA students are eligible to receive financial aid assistance from the federal government. For more information, go to RMUoHP's Financial Aid web page by clicking here.
    What advice would you give students who are interested in working part-time during the program?
    Don't do it! PA school is exceptionally rigorous. Most students who attempt to work end up quitting their jobs or dropping out of the program within a few short months after matriculating. Think about it this way... most students will end up working as Physician Assistants for 35-45 years following graduation. Are you really planning to compromise the core education you will receive during the critical 2.5-year foundational period while in school, just so you can earn what will likely be a relatively small sum of money?
    Does Provo, UT have a reasonable cost of living?
    Very reasonable! The cost-of-living index (COLI) is a measure intended to help one compare living expenses in one locale versus another. An index of 100 indicates a cost of living equal to the national average. An index of 120 indicates the need to earn an additional 20% income in order to maintain the same standard of living as a city with an index of 100. At the time of this writing (2014-04-02) the COLI for Provo, UT was 88, which is very attractive for students on a tight budget!
