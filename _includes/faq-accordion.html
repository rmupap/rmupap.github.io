• ? Length of Program
  • What is the length of the entire program?
    Eight consecutive semesters spanning 31 months.
    How many semesters are didactic and how many are clinical?
    Five didactic semesters and three clinical semesters.
    I heard you will be offering a couple of full-time clinical rotations to break up the didactic phase. Is this true? If so, why?
    Yes, it's true. Why? Because it just makes sense. This unique aspect of our program is exciting! The practice of medicine has evolved dramatically and continues to change at a rapid pace. Consequently, the educational challenge has become (much) less about memorizing mass amounts of information, and (much) more about clinical reasoning, problem-solving, and accessing, interpreting and applying high quality evidence-based information at the point-of-care. The purpose of including two full-time clinical rotations in the midst of the didactic phase is to facilitate the clinical problem-solving emphasis. This empowers students with a substantially more mature clinical perspective for the two final didactic semesters. Because they are prepared to grasp concepts of greater complexity, faculty are able to design and implement meatier learning experiences.
  • ? Degree Rewarded
  • What degree will be rewarded by the program?
    Upon successful completion of the program, Rocky Mountain University of Health Professions (RMUoHP) will award students with a Master of Physician Assistant Studies (MPAS) degree. A master's degree is considered the terminal practice degree for physician assistants.

  • ? Class Size
  • How many seats are available for the inaugural class? How about each class annually thereafter?
    Our goal is to seat 46 students for the inaugural class and 50 students each class thereafter. However, we will only seat as many as we feel confident we can accommodate with strong clinical rotations.
  • ? Prerequisites
  • Do all of the program prerequisites need to be completed prior to application?
    No. However, as described on the Prerequisite Courses page, those applicants who have outstanding prerequisite coursework at the time of application must have a feasible plan documented for completing remaining prerequisites prior to the program start date. Furthermore, applicants offered seats contingent upon successful completion of outstanding prerequisites must complete each remaining course with a grade of “B” or better.
  • ? Application Cycle
  • When will your inaugural cohort begin class?
    Our inaugural cohort will begin classes on June 1, 2015 pending successfully being awarded 'Accreditation - Provisional' in March 2015. To learn details regarding accreditation, please navigate to our Accreditation web page.
    Will you be using the Central Application Service for Physician Assistants (CASPA)?
    Yes. Interested applicants must apply through CASPA. Click or tap here to navigate to CASPA's website.
    When will the application cycle open for the inaugural class?
    Applicants should be able to select our program via the CASPA website on April 21st.
    Will there be a supplementary application?
    No.
    How many applicants do you expect will apply for the inaugural class?
    This is difficult to predict. Already there is great enthusiasm about our new program and it appears word is spreading quickly. We also know that neighboring Physician Assistant programs receive a high number of applications from residents and students in Utah Valley. Applicants who are interested in our program should not delay submitting materials via CASPA once the cycle opens. To meet our early consideration deadline of September 1st, applicants should ensure their CASPA application is completed no later than four weeks in advance (first part of August). More details are available on our Application Requirements page.
  • ? Background Check
  • Will a background investigation be required for incoming students? If so, why?
    Admittance to the program will be contingent upon a successful background check. A background check revealing information that would prohibit a student from participating in clinical rotations or prevent licensure will make him/her ineligible for the program.
  • ? Drug Screening
  • Will drug screening be performed on students?
    Yes. Enrolled students will be subject to random drug screenings at any point during the course of the program. Failure to perform or successfully pass a drug screen will render the student ineligible for clinical rotations and will thus result in dismissal from the program.
